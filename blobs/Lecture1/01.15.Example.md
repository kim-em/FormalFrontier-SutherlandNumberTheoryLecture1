**Example 1.15.** For any field $k$, the valuation $v \colon k((t)) \to \mathbb{Z} \cup \{\infty\}$ on the field of Laurent series over $k$ defined by

$$v\!\left(\sum_{n \geq n_0} a_n t^n\right) = n_0,$$

where $a_{n_0} \neq 0$, has valuation ring $k[[t]]$, the power series ring over $k$. For $f \in k((t))^{\times}$, the valuation $v(f) \in \mathbb{Z}$ is the *order of vanishing* of $f$ at zero. For every $\alpha \in k$ one can similarly define a valuation $v_{\alpha}$ on $k$ as the order of vanishing of $f$ at $\alpha$ by taking the Laurent series expansion of $f$ about $\alpha$.
