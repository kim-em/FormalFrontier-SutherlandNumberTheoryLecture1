**Example 1.24.** The ring $\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{5}]$ is not a UFD (nor a PID) because it is not integrally closed: consider $\phi = (1 + \sqrt{5})/2 \in \operatorname{Frac} \mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{5}]$, which is integral over $\mathbb{Z}$ (and hence over $\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{5}]$), since $\phi^2 - \phi - 1 = 0$. But $\phi \notin \mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{5}]$, so $\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{5}]$ is not integrally closed.

The corollary implies that every discrete valuation ring is integrally closed. In fact, more is true.
